Welcome
Let's set up your privacy gateway.
VeilNet turns your Raspberry Pi into a privacy shield for every device on your network. It blocks ads, encrypts your DNS, and can route all your traffic through a VPN — automatically, for everything, with no software to install on your phone or laptop.

This wizard will detect your hardware and walk you through the setup step by step. Most questions have a recommended answer — you can just keep clicking Next.
Before you start: make sure your Raspberry Pi is connected to your network and you can reach this page. That's it. The wizard handles everything else.
Step 1 — Network
How is your Pi connected?
This controls how much traffic VeilNet can protect. The inline setup protects every device automatically.
Inline gateway — full protection recommended
Modem → Pi → Router. Every device on your network is protected automatically. Requires a second ethernet port (USB adapter works).
DNS gateway — most devices
Pi plugged into your router. Protects devices that use your router's DNS. Easier setup, no second ethernet port needed.
Access point — wireless devices only
Pi creates its own Wi-Fi network. Only devices connected to VeilNet's Wi-Fi are protected.

Step 2 — DNS & Ad Blocking
Block ads and encrypt your DNS.
DNS is the "phone book" your devices use to find websites. By default your internet provider can read every lookup. VeilNet can block ads network-wide and make your DNS private and encrypted.
Full stack — Pi-hole + Unbound + dnscrypt-proxy recommended
Ad blocking, encrypted DNS transport, and recursive resolution from root servers. Maximum privacy — your ISP never sees your DNS queries.
Pi-hole + Unbound
Ad blocking and recursive resolution. No third-party DNS server involved at all.
Pi-hole + dnscrypt-proxy
Ad blocking plus encrypted DNS transport. Queries are encrypted but go to third-party resolvers.
Pi-hole only
Ad and tracker blocking. DNS queries still go to a provider of your choice (Cloudflare, Google, etc).
No changes
Keep your current DNS setup.
Step 3 — VPN
Route your traffic through a VPN?
A VPN encrypts all traffic leaving your network and hides your IP address from websites and your internet provider. You'll need an account with a VPN provider — we recommend Mullvad or ProtonVPN for privacy.
Mullvad

ProtonVPN

NordVPN

AirVPN

Step 4 — Security Hardening
Lock down the system.
These settings protect the Pi itself from being accessed or compromised. All are recommended — turn off anything you're certain you don't need.
SSH hardening
Disable password login, disable root access, move SSH to port 2222
nftables firewall
Strict stateful firewall — block all inbound traffic not explicitly allowed
fail2ban
Auto-ban IPs with repeated failed login attempts
Automatic security updates
Install security patches automatically — no manual apt-get needed
Important: SSH hardening will disable password login. Make sure your SSH public key is already in ~/.ssh/authorized_keys on this Pi before continuing, or you'll be locked out after reboot.
